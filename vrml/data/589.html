Unique Qualities

Staff: A multi layered linked programme of work that includes participatory , professional standard gallery work and production facilities. The balance of disabled and non disabled staff provides an equality of input but also of expectation.

Board: Dynamic is a good word for QArts, its an incredibly imaginative organisation. One important thing is that it has such a lot of experienced staff, the Director and Programme Manager have so much experience in QArts and in other organisations. There is a strong body of experience which, in one organisation, is is very unique. But there is also space for new experience and ideas to come in giving scope to the organisation.
QArts has vision, they are trying to do something imaginative by running a really good gallery programme, and it tries to make links with the different strands of its work. There is a buzz about the organisation as though it is going places.
The experience of people at the top can bring about high standards of work, they have the skills and experience to do something exciting and imaginative.

Linked: Q Arts work in a genuinely consultative manner. Their work is guided by the ideas and needs of the communities or organisations they are working with;it is a genuine partnership. Focussing on accessibility and regeneration within community groups and areas Q Arts projects always result in work of a very high standard.

Participant: The atmosphere is welcoming, the people are friendly and the approach to work is very good. There is lots of support for disabled participants to run their own projects.

Participant: Being in the forefront of innovative participatory arts practice. The equality in management structure, this goes beyond the administrative base and includes their attitude towards disabled members of staff.|There is a constant process of evaluation and updating, always pushing to improve what they do. Here everyone can have their say.|The supportive structure of training that can take you from volunteer to paid work.
